# Certification Readiness Rubric

Synthetic knowledge source for CertForge Sentinel.

## Purpose

This rubric defines how CertForge Sentinel evaluates whether a learner appears ready for a certification attempt. It is synthetic and created for hackathon demonstration only.

## Readiness Levels

### Ready

A learner may be considered Ready when they meet most of the following conditions:

- Practice assessment score is 80% or higher
- At least 20 focused study hours completed
- Weak topics are limited or recently reviewed
- Learner can explain core concepts clearly
- Learner has completed at least one full practice assessment
- No major certification domains are untouched

Recommended next step:

- Schedule the exam or complete one final review session.

### Almost Ready

A learner may be considered Almost Ready when they meet some of the following conditions:

- Practice assessment score is between 70% and 79%
- At least 12 to 19 focused study hours completed
- One or two weak domains remain
- Learner understands most core concepts but needs review
- Practice assessment performance is improving

Recommended next step:

- Complete targeted review on weak topics and retake a practice assessment.

### Not Yet Ready

A learner may be considered Not Yet Ready when they meet several of the following conditions:

- Practice assessment score is below 70%
- Fewer than 12 focused study hours completed
- Multiple weak domains remain
- Learner has not completed a full practice assessment
- Learner cannot explain several core concepts
- Study schedule is inconsistent or overloaded

Recommended next step:

- Continue structured study and focus on foundational concepts before attempting the exam.

## Risk Factors

The following factors may increase readiness risk:

- High meeting load
- Low focus time
- Short deadline
- Multiple weak topics
- Low practice score
- Inconsistent study history
- No recent review of missed questions

## Support Recommendations

For individual learners:

- Prioritize weak topics first
- Use short daily study blocks
- Complete practice questions after each topic
- Review missed answers
- Schedule a readiness check before the exam

For managers:

- Reduce nonessential meeting load when possible
- Protect focus time for learners
- Group learners by certification goal
- Review team-level weak domains
- Offer additional support for high-risk learners
