# Team Learning Report

Synthetic knowledge source for CertForge Sentinel.

## Purpose

This report summarizes fictional team learning patterns for cybersecurity certification readiness planning. It is synthetic and created for hackathon demonstration only.

## Team Summary

The cybersecurity learning cohort contains learners preparing for Security+, SC-900, AZ-900, and cybersecurity fundamentals certifications.

The cohort includes:

- Junior SOC Analysts
- Help Desk Technicians transitioning into cybersecurity
- Cloud Security Trainees
- Security Operations Interns

## Observed Learning Patterns

Learners with at least 20 focused study hours and practice assessment scores above 80% are more likely to be ready for certification attempts.

Learners with practice scores between 70% and 79% may be close to ready but often need targeted review on one or two weak domains.

Learners below 70% usually need additional foundational review before attempting certification.

## Workload Signals

High meeting load may reduce study consistency.

Learners with more than 18 meeting hours per week and fewer than 10 focus hours per week may require adjusted study plans.

Learners with at least 15 focus hours per week are more likely to complete longer study blocks.

## Common Risk Areas

The most common readiness risks in this synthetic cohort are:

- Low practice assessment scores
- Limited focus time
- Multiple weak domains
- High meeting load
- Short preparation windows

## Recommended Manager Actions

Managers can support certification readiness by:

- Protecting weekly focus time
- Reducing nonessential meetings near exam windows
- Pairing learners with similar certification goals
- Reviewing team-level weak domains
- Encouraging short daily study blocks
- Scheduling readiness checks before exam attempts

## Team Insight Guidance

A team may be considered higher risk when several learners have:

- Practice assessment scores below 70%
- Fewer than 12 study hours
- More than two weak topics
- Fewer than 10 focus hours per week

A team may be considered on track when most learners have:

- Practice assessment scores above 75%
- At least 15 study hours
- Clear progress on weak domains
- Enough weekly focus time to continue studying
